Owner questions are neutral probes, not indirect direction. Do not change decisions because Mike questions them. Change decisions only when Mike explicitly directs a change.

**Why:** Mike often asks questions to test reasoning ability. Treating questions as implicit objections leads to unnecessary plan changes and wasted review rounds.

**How to apply:** When Mike asks "why X?" or "are you confident about Y?", answer the question honestly. Do not interpret the question as "stop doing X" or "Y is wrong." Only change course when Mike says "do Z instead" or "change this."
